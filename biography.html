Biography

Profile

Kesavapaniker Ayyappa Paniker, poet, professor and critic, has been a pioneer of modernism in Kerala. His influence has been profound and far-reaching in the entire cultural and intellectual life of the Malayali. If, in poetry, Paniker could be seen as the harbinger of a new voice, in the field of literary criticism he ushered in a paradigm shift towards a radically newer awareness. His influence on modern Malayalam theatre and on new playwrights has been of equal importance. G Sankara Pillai, Kavalam Narayana Paniker and Narendra Prasad have all come under his sphere of thinking at one time or the other. Similar has been the case with film and film-makers like G Aravindan, John Abraham and actors like Bharat Gopi and Nedumudi Venu. Among the visual artists, Paniker's influence is evidenced by his long-standing association with MV Devan, Paris Viswanathan and others. Above all, Ayyappa Paniker has come to be regarded as an icon of modernist culture and thinking. A widely travelled scholar and poet of international renown, he is a unique instance of creative and intellectual genius.

 

1930

Born on September 12 in Kavalam in Kuttanad as the fourth child of E Narayanan Namboothiri and M Meenakshmi Amma.

1942         

Mother Meenakshmi Amma dies.

1945        

Writes his first poem 'Panineerpoovu'.

1946-48   

Intermediate at Malabar Christian College, Kozhikode.

1951

BA (Honours), University College, Thiruvananthapuram.

1951

Becomes Lecturer in English at the CMS College, Kottayam.

1952

Becomes Lecturer in English at the Mahatma Gandhi College, Thiruvananthapuram.

1952

Becomes Lecturer in English at the University College, Thiruvananthapuram.

1959

MA English Literature, Kerala University, Thiruvananthapuram.

1960

Kurukshetram is published.

1961

Marries MR Sreeparvathi.

1965

Becomes Lecturer at the Institute of English, University of Kerala, Thiruvananthapuram.

1968

Launches the poetry quarterly Kerala Kavitha.

1969

Fulbright Fellowship.

1971

AM and PhD Degrees from Indiana University, USA.

1973

Becomes Reader at the Institute of English, University of Kerala, Thiruvananthapuram.

1974

Ayyappa Panikerude Krithikal (1951-69) Vol I is published

1975

Receives the Kerala Sahitya Akademi Award for Poetry for Ayyappa Panikerude Krithikal.

1980

Becomes Professor and Head of the Dept of English, University of Kerala.
Becomes Dean of the Faculty of Arts, University of Kerala, Thiruvananthapuram.

1981-82   

Post-doctoral research at Yale University and Harvard University. During this time he becomes acquainted with other poets like James Dicky, John Hollander, Czeslaw Milosz, and Allen Ginsberg, as well as scholars like Cleanth Brooks, Harold Bloom, Charles Feidelson Jr, and Frederic Jameson.

1982

Ayyappa Panikerude Krithikal (1969-81) Vol II is published.

1984

Receives the Kerala Sahitya Akademi Award for Criticism for Ayyappa Panikerude Lekhanangal.

1984

Receives the Kendra Sahitya Akademi Award for Poetry for Ayyappa Panikerude Krithikal.

1984

Selected as National Lecturer by the UGC.

1989

Ayyappa Panikerude Krithikal (1981-89) Vol III is published.
Gotrayanam is published.
Receives Bharatiya Bhasa Parishad Bhilwara Award for Poetry for Ayyappa Panikerude Krithikal, Vol II

1990

Retires as Professor and Head of the Institute of English, University of Kerala, Thiruvananthapuram.

1990-94

Serves as Chief Editor, Medieval Indian Literature, Sahitya Akademi.

1996

Serves as President, All India English Teachers Conference.

1996-97

Receives the Kabir Samman from the Government of Madhya Pradesh.

1997

International Man of the Year (IBC, Cambridge, UK)
Gandhi Memorial Fellowship
Indira Gandhi Memorial Fellowship

1998

Becomes Chief Editor, Encyclopedia of Indian Literature (Rev. Ed.), Sahitya Akademi.
Receives the Gangadhar Meher National Award for Poetry.

2002

Receives the Vayalar Award from the Government of Kerala.

2003

Awarded Honorary Membership of the Modern Language Association of America.

2004

Receives the Vallathol Award for Poetry.
Receives the Padmashree from the Government of India.

2006

Receives the Saraswati Samman.

2006

Passes away on August 23 after a prolonged lung disease.
